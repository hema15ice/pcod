About PCOD

Understanding Polycystic Ovarian Disease

What is PCOD?

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is a common hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age.

It's characterized by multiple small cysts in the ovaries along with hormonal imbalances that can cause irregular periods, excess androgen levels, and various metabolic issues.

While the exact cause is unknown, PCOD is believed to result from a combination of genetic and environmental factors.

Common Symptoms

  • Irregular menstrual cycles
  • Excessive hair growth (hirsutism)
  • Acne and oily skin
  • Weight gain and difficulty losing weight
  • Hair thinning or male-pattern baldness
  • Skin darkening (acanthosis nigricans)

Why Early Detection Matters

Early diagnosis and management of PCOD can help prevent long-term complications such as:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Heart disease
  • Endometrial cancer
  • Infertility
  • Depression and anxiety

Managing PCOD

Diet & Nutrition

A balanced diet low in refined carbs and high in fiber can help manage insulin resistance and weight.

Exercise

Regular physical activity helps improve insulin sensitivity and aids in weight management.

Medical Treatment

Depending on symptoms, treatments may include hormonal therapy, insulin-sensitizing drugs, or fertility treatments.
